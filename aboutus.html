About Us

Our Mission

At Kalmont our mission is to provide rapid, skilled, and compassionate search and rescue services in times of crisis. Committed to saving lives, we operate with professionalism, teamwork, and resilience, ensuring the safety of those in need—whether in the wilderness, mountains, urban environments, or at sea. Through continuous training, cutting-edge technology, and collaboration with local and national agencies, we strive to respond swiftly and effectively to emergencies. Our team stands ready 24/7 to serve our community with integrity, courage, and an unwavering dedication to bringing people home.

Who are our members?

Our team is composed of dedicated volunteers from diverse backgrounds, each bringing unique skills and expertise to our search and rescue operations. Many of our members are outdoor enthusiasts, including skiers, climbers, and mountaineers who understand the challenges of mountain environments.

We have medical professionals, including doctors, nurses, and paramedics, who provide critical care in emergency situations. Technical specialists with expertise in rope systems, avalanche forecasting, and navigation ensure we can handle complex rescue scenarios.

All members undergo rigorous training in wilderness first aid, technical rescue techniques, and survival skills. We maintain high standards through regular training exercises and certifications, ensuring we're always prepared to respond effectively to emergencies in the mountains.

Avalanche

Avalanches can happen whenever there is snow lying on the ground at a sufficient angle. Every year there are avalanche accidents in Whistler. While there is always an avalanche risk in winter, some of these accidents could have been avoided by awareness and planning.

Hypothermia

The two most common causes of hypothermia on the hill are exhaustion and injury. Walkers with inadequate food, clothing and shelter will not be able to sustain the level of physical work to replace the heat they are losing. Injury leads to immobility and a similar inability to generate heat – just think how you cool, off when you stop for your 'piece'.
